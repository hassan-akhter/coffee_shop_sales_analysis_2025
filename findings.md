# Key Findings: Coffee Shop Sales Analysis 2025

A summary of the most important insights discovered through the SQL analysis and Python visualizations of the 2025 coffee shop sales data across 15 stores in 5 countries.

## Revenue

1. **Manchester was the top-performing store**, generating the highest annual revenue across all 15 stores — edging out Paris and Wroclaw which came in second and third respectively.

2. **February was the weakest month across every city without exception.** Every single store dipped in February, suggesting a seasonal pattern that the business should plan promotions around.

3. **Total annual revenue across all 15 stores reached approximately 7 million**, with remarkably low variance between cities — the gap between the highest and lowest performing store was less than 5%, indicating a well-balanced and consistently performing chain.


## Products

4. **Beans is the dominant revenue category**, accounting for the largest share of total product revenue — significantly ahead of Espresso in second place. This suggests the chain's core business strength lies in retail coffee bean sales rather than prepared drinks alone.

5. **Latte is the best-selling product by units**, topping the product ranking across the full year. Chai Latte and Blueberry Muffin follow closely, showing that customers pair drinks with food consistently.

6. **The Pumpkin Spice Latte showed clear seasonal demand**, with units sold rising noticeably in autumn months. This validates the value of keeping seasonal items in the menu rotation and suggests an opportunity to expand the seasonal range.


## Customers

7. **Bronze tier customers generate the majority of total revenue** despite being the lowest loyalty tier. This is a volume effect — there are far more Bronze customers than Gold, meaning retention and upselling within this tier represents the biggest revenue growth opportunity.

8. **The top 20 customers by lifetime value are spread across all three loyalty tiers**, with Gold tier customers not exclusively dominating the top spots. This suggests the loyalty programme may need rebalancing to better reward the highest spenders.

9. **Repeat customer rate is high**, indicating strong customer retention across the chain. The business is better at keeping existing customers than it might need to focus on acquiring new ones.


## Operations

10. **Peak ordering activity falls consistently in the morning hours**, which is expected for a coffee shop chain but confirms that staffing, stock preparation, and promotional timing should all be concentrated in the pre-noon window to maximise efficiency and customer satisfaction.


## Summary

The business is performing consistently well across all markets with stable revenue, strong product variety, and a loyal customer base. The key opportunities for growth are: targeted February promotions to offset the seasonal dip, loyalty programme optimisation to convert Bronze customers into higher tiers, and expanding the seasonal drinks menu based on the Pumpkin Spice Latte's strong performance.
